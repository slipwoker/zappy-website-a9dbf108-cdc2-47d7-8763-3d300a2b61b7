Frequently Asked Questions

Clear answers to common questions about our real estate legal services.

Do I need an attorney to buy a home?

While not legally required in all jurisdictions, having an experienced real estate attorney protects your interests throughout the transaction. We review contracts, identify potential issues, and ensure the title is clear before you close.

What does title review involve?

Our title review examines the property's ownership history to uncover any liens, easements, or encumbrances. We verify the seller has the legal right to transfer the property and work to resolve any defects before closing.

How do you handle real estate disputes?

We pursue resolution through negotiation and mediation whenever possible, but we are fully prepared to litigate boundary disputes, contract breaches, and title claims. Our goal is to protect your property rights efficiently and effectively.

What is due diligence?

Due diligence is the investigation period where we verify every aspect of the property — zoning, environmental conditions, survey boundaries, and existing leases. It's your opportunity to uncover any issues before committing to the purchase.

How much do your services cost?

We offer transparent, competitive pricing tailored to the scope of your matter. Whether you need a simple contract review or full litigation support, we provide a clear fee structure upfront so there are no surprises.

What areas do you serve?

Keystone Legal serves clients across the state, with a primary focus on metropolitan and suburban real estate markets. Contact our office to confirm coverage in your specific county or region.
